## 1. Property Issue Summary

The property, Flat No. 103, is experiencing widespread dampness and active leakage issues across several internal areas, including the Hall, Common Bedroom, Master Bedroom, Kitchen, and the ceiling of the Common Bathroom. Significant water seepage is also evident in the Parking Area located directly below Flat No. 103.

The primary causes appear to be:

- **Internal Plumbing & Waterproofing Defects:** Issues within Flat No. 103's Common and Master Bedroom Bathrooms, specifically defective tile joints, loose plumbing connections, suspected concealed plumbing leaks, and damage to the underlying waterproofing layers (Nahani trap/Brickbat coba).
- **External Envelope Issues:** Moderate cracks and defects in the building's external walls and external plumbing pipes, particularly near the Master Bedroom.
- **Upper Floor Leakage:** Water intrusion from the flat directly above (Flat No. 203) affecting the Common Bathroom ceiling of Flat No. 103.

Additionally, moderate cracks have been observed in some Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC) structural elements (Columns and Beams). Thermal imaging was conducted, but the provided report did not contain specific diagnostic data to further analyze these issues.

## 3. Probable Root Cause

The widespread dampness in the skirting areas of the Hall, Common Bedroom, Master Bedroom, and Kitchen within Flat No. 103 is primarily due to water escaping from the adjacent Common and Master Bedroom Bathrooms of Flat No. 103.

This is likely caused by:

- **Defective Waterproofing & Tile Issues:** Gaps in tile joints, tile hollowness, and probable damage to the Nahani trap/Brickbat coba (the waterproofing layer beneath the tiles) allow water to seep from the bathroom floors into the surrounding walls.
- **Plumbing Leaks:** Loose plumbing connections and suspected concealed plumbing leaks contribute to continuous water ingress and seepage in the parking ceiling below.
- **External Moisture Intrusion:** Cracks in external walls and leaking external plumbing pipes enable moisture to penetrate the building envelope.
- **Neighboring Property Leakage:** Leakage from Flat No. 203 bathrooms contributes to dampness observed in the Common Bathroom ceiling of Flat No. 103.

---

## 4. Severity Assessment

**Internal Dampness & Leakage (Hall, Bedrooms, Kitchen, Parking Seepage): HIGH**

Reasoning: Multiple areas are affected by ongoing water intrusion, indicating persistent plumbing and waterproofing failures. This can lead to structural deterioration, mold growth, and interior damage if not addressed promptly.

**Common Bathroom Ceiling Dampness (Leakage from Flat No. 203): MODERATE**

Reasoning: Localized leakage originating from the upper floor contributes to moisture accumulation and requires coordination with the neighboring unit.

**External Wall & RCC Cracks: MODERATE**

Reasoning: Cracks in the building envelope allow water ingress and may worsen structural deterioration over time. Structural inspection is recommended.

---

## 5. Recommended Actions

1. **Rectify Bathroom Leakage (Flat No. 103)**  
   - Repair defective tile joints and Nahani trap joints.  
   - Investigate concealed plumbing leaks.  
   - Replace hollow tiles and restore waterproofing layers.

2. **Repair External Wall Defects**  
   - Seal external wall cracks and apply waterproof coating.  
   - Repair or replace leaking external plumbing pipes.  
   - Remove algae and fungus growth before repainting.

3. **Address Leakage from Flat No. 203**  
   - Coordinate with the neighboring unit to repair tile joints and plumbing leaks causing water intrusion.

4. **Conduct Structural Assessment**  
   - Engage a structural engineer to evaluate cracks in RCC columns and beams.

5. **Restore Internal Finishes**  
   - After leak sources are resolved, remove damaged plaster and paint.  
   - Apply damp-proofing treatments before repainting.
